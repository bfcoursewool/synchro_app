99% of nootropic products are BORING. Synchro Cognos is different.

Here’s the dirty secret of the cognitive-enhancement (aka “nootropic”) industry:
99% of products on the market today are just slight variations of the same conventional formula.

These products intend to put the brain into a hyper-stimulated, narrowly-focused, “process-oriented” state. In practical terms, if you’re wanting to hammer out some data-entry or clean out your email inbox, conventional nootropic products could work great for you. In our humble opinion this state is BORING and relatively low-value.

In developing Synchro Cognos, we were after something different; the genuinely inspired states of “flow” from which great ideas are born. And realistically, conventional nootropics are more likely to hinder you than be of use in realizing these high-value states.

We knew that it was possible to formulate a nootropic product that would reliably produce creative flow states, and do it in a way that was actually healthy for the brain. The task simply required someone with a deep knowledge of brain and body metabolism to be innovative and think creatively about the formula design process. (Thankfully, that someone was us)

Synchro Cognos creates the ideal conditions for creative flow states through the synergistic interaction of three distinct components:

Components

1Anti-inflammatory component

Taking a holistic look at the brain and body, it was clear that the single biggest factor suppressing both energy levels and brain function for most people was systemic inflammation. Trying to improve cognitive performance without first addressing inflammation is like putting race fuel in a fundamentally broken engine. Cognos utilizes nature’s most potent inflammation-reducer (turmeric extract) to address this issue first and foremost.

2Flow Component

This is where the magic comes in. Cognos uses a meticulously formulated set of amino acids and plant extracts (L-Theanine, Phenibut, Mucuna) designed to relax the brain and nervous system and facilitate the shift into a state of focused creative flow.

3Nutrient-Delivery Component

Again taking a holistic view of the body-brain system, we saw that there was frequently a basic nutrient-availability problem (i.e. oxygen and glucose and/or ketones) in the brain that limited cognitive performance. Cognos uses a blend of vitamins and naturally-occurring compounds (Theobromine, Pyridoxine) to stimulate blood-flow and increasing delivery of these critical nutrients to the brain.

Who is Synchro Cognos for?

Where is Synchro Cognos made?

Where is Synchro based?
Synchro was founded in 2011 by biochemist and nutritionist Graham Ryan with the mission of helping others pursue a state of true health. Synchro is headquartered in and ships all orders from Berkeley, CA.

How does Synchro Cognos work?
A synergistic interaction of three key functional components (enhanced nutrient delivery, neural inflammation reduction, flow-state catalysis) allow Synchro Cognos to create the ideal metabolic and neurohormonal conditions for sustained, focused creativity.

How do I use Synchro Cognos?
For facilitation of creative flow states, take 3 capsules with your first cup of coffee or tea in a day. (pairing with caffeine is recommended, but not required)
Ideal dosage will vary depending on the individual and can be increased to 4-5 capsules if needed. Cognos can be used daily, but delivers maximum benefit when used not more than 2 days consecutively or 4 days in a 7 day period.

When can I expect results?
Most people experience meaningful improvements in focus and creativity within one hour of taking Synchro Cognoss. If your experience does not meet these expectations, try increasing dosage by one capsule the next time you use Cognos.

Can I take Synchro Cognos with my prescription medication?
Synchro Cognos is extremely safe, but there are a few potential interactions to be mindful of. Cognos should not be combined with alcohol, MAOIs, SSRIs, antidepressants, and benzodiazepines (Xanax, Klonopin, Valium, etc) as it could increase potency of these drugs. If you are prescribed antidepressants, we recommend checking in with your doctor before beginning use of Cognos.
